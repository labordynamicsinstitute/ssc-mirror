help nytimes
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Title


    nytimes -- Top news stories from the New York Times




Syntax


        nytimes [, feed(feedname) number(#)]


Description


    nytimes displays items from New York Times RSS feeds. It requires a
    connection to the internet.








Options


    feed(feedname) allows the user to specify which New York Times feed will
        be displayed. The default is the NYTimes.com Home Page (U.S.).  As of
        January 2011, other feed possiblities included: global home; news
        sites focusing on the world, Africa, the Americas, Asia Pacific,
        Europe, Middle East; US news feeds focusing on the US, education,
        politics, NY region, and energy environment; business news feeds
        focusing on global business, small business, economy, media and
        advertising, and your money; technology news feeds focusing on
        business-computing, internet, personal tech, and start-ups; sports
        news feeds focusing on sports, global sports, baseball, college
        basketball, college football, golf, hockey, pro basketball, pro
        football, soccer, and tennis; science news feeds focusing on science,
        environment, space, health research, nutrition, health care policy;
        and many more.  A complete list is available.


    number(#) allows the user to specify how many feeds will displayed. The
        default is 5.


Remarks
    This command is a very crude RSS reader for feeds produced by the New
    York Times. It reports the headline, which is a hyperlink to the full
    text of the newspaper article, a brief article summary, and the date and
    time the article was published. Clicking on a headline will open up the
    article in your default web browser.


    If you want a number of feed items different than five, you may include
    the number as option. For example nytimes, n(10) will return the ten more
    recent items.  Normally, the Times feed only reports about a dozen items
    which creates an upper limit for the number of story summaries that will
    be displayed.


    nytimes doesn't work well for NYTimes.com blogs, as it is not set up to
    parse the ones that are routed through Feedburner. Some blog feeds will
    work, some will not.


    nytimes has no knowledge of whether or not you have seen a particular
    item before, so it will repeat items if you make multiple calls to it
    over a short period of time.


Examples


        . nytimes


        . nytimes, feed(arts)


        . nytimes, n(1)


        . nytimes, f(sports) n(3)
