Turnbull Nonparametric Estimator for Williness to Pay




        turnbull {bid} {t}


    where


    bid is the variable that reports the bid value offered to the respondent.


    t is the variable that indicates if the repondent would like to
    contribute to the project at the price offered (T =1 if WTP>AMOUNT).


Description


    turnbull estimates the Turnbull empirical distribution estimator of
    willingness to pay proposed by Carson et al (1994a,b). Haab and McConnell
    (1997) argue that this estimator solves the problem of estimating
    negative willingness to pay without resorting to ad hoc distribution
    assumptions, and show that central tendency measures of willingness to
    pay from parametric models are sensitive to the assumed distribution,
    while the lower bound Turnbull estimate is robust accross distributions.


Examples:


    . use ferry dataset (http://www-sekon.slu.se/~bkr/)
   
    . turnbull bid t
   
    . turnbull bid t, short
